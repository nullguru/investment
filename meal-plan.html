B. Key Food Rules

Low salt, low sugar, low oil. Avoid processed foods, pickle, papad, namkeen, and salty marinades.
Low saturated fat and liver-friendly cooking. Prefer curd, dal in controlled portions, vegetables, phulka, oats, daliya, and khichdi.
Keep mutton out today. Keep chicken optional only for others, since it does not fit the pregnant eater's preference today.

1 Best Fit Shared base meal

Egg Curry + Light Khichdi Day

The cleanest fit for everyone: controlled carbs, low purine, mild flavors, and fully cooked eggs.

Breakfast
Vegetable oats upma + curd + boiled egg

Use carrots, beans, peas, capsicum. Add 1 fully boiled egg for those who want it.

Lunch
Mild boiled egg curry + phulka + bhindi or beans

Tomato-onion gravy, low oil, low salt, no packaged masala. Add cucumber curd on the side.

Dinner
Moong dal vegetable khichdi + curd

Lauki, carrot, beans, or spinach. Keep it soft, light, and early.

Why this works

Low glycemic, high fiber, low saturated fat, low purine, pregnancy-safe, and no chicken conflict.

2 Variety Fiber rich

Chilla + Small Rajma + Bhurji Bowl

A tasty vegetarian-egg day with fiber and protein, but rajma should be limited for high uric acid.

Breakfast
Besan vegetable chilla + curd

Add grated lauki, carrot, onion, coriander, and mint chutney. Cook with minimal oil.

Lunch
Small rajma bowl + phulka or small brown rice

Keep rajma quantity modest. Pair with cooked vegetables and curd.

Dinner
Fully cooked egg bhurji bowl + sauteed vegetables

Serve with 1 phulka or small millet roti. Keep spice mild.

Why this works

Good satiety and variety, with enough protein for fitness goals. It is less ideal than Option 1 because legumes need portion control.

3 Conditional Split lunch

Idli + Optional Chicken Lunch + Daliya

Use this if the family wants non-veg, with a separate egg or curd plate for the pregnant eater.

Breakfast
Idli + vegetable sambar + small chutney

Keep chutney small and sambar vegetable-heavy.

Lunch
Tandoori-style chicken for others + egg plate for her

No salty marinade. Serve with phulka, sabzi, and curd. Pregnant eater gets egg curry or curd bowl.

Dinner
Vegetable daliya + clear egg-drop soup

Keep the soup fully cooked and mild. Skip soup if dinner needs to be simpler.

Why this works

It gives variety and a non-veg option, but it creates extra handling and is not as smooth for uric acid or pregnancy preference.
