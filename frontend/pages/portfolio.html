① Blueprint

Portfolio Theory — How We Build

Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)

Harry Markowitz (1952): combine assets that don't move together to get the same return at lower risk. The "efficient frontier" is the set of portfolios with the best risk/return trade-off. Key insight: diversification is the only free lunch in investing.

Stock Concentration

Maximum 8% in any single stock. With 15–18 stocks you get ~90% of the diversification benefit of an index. Beyond 20–25 stocks, incremental diversification is minimal.

Sharia Constraints

Sharia screening excludes financials (~30% of Nifty) and some cyclicals, creating a natural tilt toward IT, healthcare, industrials, and consumer goods — a tilt that has historically outperformed in high-growth markets. The policy targets below account for this.

The 55 / 30 / 15 Cap Mix

55% Large Cap · 30% Mid Cap · 15% Small Cap. Large caps provide stability; mid caps offer growth at moderate risk; small caps add return potential but must be capped to control volatility.

Sector Diversification

No single sector above 15% of portfolio. Each target sector below has a weight band — staying within it prevents overexposure to any one economic cycle.

Rebalancing Rule

Rebalance by deploying new money first (add to underweight) before selling — minimises churn and tax events. Sell only when a stock exceeds 12% or a sector exceeds 20%.
